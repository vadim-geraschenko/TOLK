# TOLK Visual Direction

## Stage Status
Approved by user on April 18, 2026 with adjustments:
- minimalist halos and ornaments are allowed;
- frames and contours may be used more actively;
- baseline motion language may include subtle floating, breathing, glow, and restrained fire / thunder accents.

## Direction Source
Selected direction: `Option 1 — Nocturnal Editorial`

TOLK should feel like a dark editorial-cultural object with clean tonal contrast, not like a startup landing page, occult product, church portal, or AI-generated spectacle. The site should read as intelligent, composed, and slightly dramatic, with atmosphere built through typography, framing, negative space, and measured motion.

## Direction Summary
The visual identity is grounded in charcoal and graphite surfaces, restrained silver-white accents, and clean pale typography. The overall mood is nocturnal, reflective, and editorial. Religious imagery may appear, but only in distilled form: halos, circular motifs, ornamental lines, and sacred light should be reduced to precise graphic gestures rather than literal decorative symbolism.

This direction should support three simultaneous readings:
- a serious media archive;
- a cultural object with taste and restraint;
- a living project with subtle motion and emotional temperature.

## Visual Character
- Dark, layered, and calm rather than flat-black and severe.
- Cinematic rather than technological.
- Premium rather than luxurious-for-show.
- Symbolic rather than decorative.
- Human-curated rather than trend-driven.
- Cleaner and drier rather than smoky, molten, or “mystical premium”.

## Core Atmosphere
- Textured charcoal / graphite background with visible depth.
- Silver-white used as the main accent and contour language.
- Very restrained warm notes may appear rarely, but never dominate the interface.
- Strong editorial typography with generous spacing and vertical rhythm.
- Framed content blocks that feel intentional and composed.
- Motion that feels ambient, not performative.

## Motion Language
The motion system should support atmosphere and hierarchy rather than novelty.

Allowed baseline motion:
- subtle vertical floating for select sacred / atmospheric layers;
- very slow breathing motion for highlighted objects or framed media;
- soft inner glow shifts on hover or focus;
- controlled ember-like or storm-like accent movement in rare key moments;
- quiet scroll-linked reveals with no hard snapping or flashy transitions.

Motion should remain sparse and low-amplitude. The interface should never feel game-like, magical, or over-animated.

## Use of Symbolic Religious Forms
Religious motifs are permitted only in reduced, graphic form.

Allowed:
- thin halos or circular linework;
- restrained ornamental borders;
- soft aureole-like light fields;
- radial or arched framing derived from icon / manuscript geometry;
- faint sacred-light cues in hero or special sections.

Avoid:
- ornate church styling;
- heavy iconographic collage;
- faux-Byzantine pattern density;
- mystical symbols that imply esotericism;
- literal “heaven fantasy” visuals spread across the full site.

## About Page Special Treatment
The `About / О проекте` page may contain a large atmospheric video layer featuring the angelic figure from `reference_2.mp4`. The preferred use is as a narrative background system tied to scroll progress, where the video advances with reading progression.

Design intent:
- explanatory content lives in structured foreground blocks;
- the video sits behind or between content bands as a cinematic field;
- the page should feel authored, not gimmicky;
- text legibility must remain intact at every scroll state;
- the rest of the site should not depend on this treatment.

This page is a special mode inside the design system, not the universal page template.

## Optional Character Layer
The animated figure from `reference_3.gif` may be integrated later as a secondary atmospheric device.

Permitted behaviors:
- hovering above a featured block;
- drifting subtly in negative space;
- acting as a rare cursor-following accent in a contained region.

Constraints:
- never central in hierarchy;
- never always-on across all pages;
- never cute, playful, or mascot-like in tone;
- should feel like an editorial artifact or symbolic watcher, not a cartoon assistant.

## Three System Priorities
1. Editorial clarity
The site must remain readable, structured, and content-led.

2. Atmospheric depth
The site should feel dimensional and memorable through tone, light, and careful framing.

3. Restraint
Every symbolic or animated gesture must feel earned.

## Do
- use light, type, and framing as primary expressive tools;
- build dark backgrounds from layered graphite values rather than pure black;
- use silver-white contour and pale light as the primary accent system;
- keep warm accents rare, secondary, and carefully controlled;
- allow more frames, contours, and fine lines than in a typical modern editorial site;
- keep cards, covers, and sections visually composed and slightly ceremonial;
- reserve motion-rich treatments for hero moments, `About`, and select featured modules;
- keep the result adult, thoughtful, and quietly dramatic.

## Don’t
- do not build a church-themed decorative interface;
- do not turn the site into a mystical / occult / pseudo-spiritual artifact;
- do not use excessive blur, bloom, metallic glow, or soft-focus fantasy imagery;
- do not overuse glitch, thunder, flame, or floating elements;
- do not make the optional character behave like a mascot or novelty widget;
- do not flatten the interface into generic monochrome minimalism.
- do not treat “premium” as gold-heavy styling or pseudo-luxury gloss.

## Assumptions
- Primary platform is responsive web.
- The site should feel more editorial-cultural than app-like.
- The home page remains the central editorial entry point.
- `About` gets a special immersive treatment, while the rest of the site stays more restrained.
- The visual system must support archives, cards, events, support content, and featured media without losing coherence.

## Handoff Notes For Design Tokens
The token stage should define:
- a charcoal-first palette with silver-white accents and precise pale neutrals;
- frame, contour, and line treatments as first-class UI primitives;
- hover and glow rules based on subtle inner illumination, not generic shadows;
- typography with editorial authority and long-form readability;
- dedicated motion tokens for ambient float, breathing, and restrained glow pulse;
- a special token group for cinematic media sections and scroll-synced video backgrounds;
- a reserved token slot for optional symbolic character layers.
